This is a privilege that comes with legal obligations for the club and participating members.

CPS Requirements:

Applicants must be financial members of the JCCA, (JCCA is legally required to notify VicRoads within 7 days of any membership lapse, which will render the car unregistered)

Cars registered on the CPS must be registered in the name of the member.

Members can have a non-Jensen vehicle on the CPS providing they already have a Jensen registered on the scheme through JCCA. However, the JCCA has limited this to vehicles requiring road worthy certificates (RWC), thereby excluding vintage and veteran cars of other marques due to the expertise required to certify them. 

Members must correctly display their current windscreen label and ensure the logbook and permit are filled in and carried in the vehicle at all times when the vehicle is in use.

Once members receive their signed paperwork back, they must submit it to VicRoads for payment (including RWC for new applications).

Promptly advise JCCA and VicRoads of any change of contact details.

CPS New Application Requirements

Applicants must allow sufficient time for application forms to be processed by an authorised JCCA officer and include the following:

Current Roadworthy Certificate.

Recent high quality images of the car (VicRoads requirement) emailed to cpsvic@jcca.org.au

.       Front and rear of car .
.       Driver’s side of the car 
.       Driving position with driver’s door ajar 
.       Engine number
.       Chassis number

A completed VicRoads Vehicle Eligibility and Standards declaration for club permit vehicles form.

Any additional documentation mandated by VicRoads eg. Vehicle Assessment Signatory Scheme (VASS) for modified cars.

Include a stamped return addressed envelope

CPS Renewal Requirements

Include a stamped return addressed envelope •

To ensure our records are up to date, existing CPS members may be required to provide current photos if those that we have are deemed out of date.
