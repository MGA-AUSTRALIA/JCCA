NSW Historic Registration





The JCCA is now a member of the NSW Historic Vehicle Scheme and can authorise historic vehicle plates (club plates) for NSW JCCA members.
This means NSW members can benefit from much reduced registration and insurance costs –
enjoy club runs with all the clubs the car is registered to - and still use your Jensen for 60 additional days per year!

NSW members can register their cars with the JCCA as the Primary Club or Secondary Club.
As Primary Club, JCCA is responsible for administration of the NSW HVS system for your Jensen, whereas if your Jensen is registered with the JCCA as a Secondary Club, it just means
you can attend JCCA events without using up your 60 day allowance.

NSW Historic Vehicle Scheme (HVS) Rules;

The Jensen Car Club Inc (JCCA) will administer the NSW Historic Vehicle Scheme (HVS) for eligible vehicles as per these Rules.

Applicants must be financial members of the JCCA, (JCCA is required to notify VicRoads within 7 days of membership lapse which will render the car unregistered

Cars registered on the CPS must be registered in the name of the member.

Members can have non Jensen vehicles on the CPS providing they already have a Jensen registered on the scheme through JCCA limited to vehicles requiring roadworthy certificates, thereby excluding Vintage and Veteran cars of other Marques due to the expertise required to certify them.

Members must display current windscreen label and ensure the log book and permit are filled out and carried in the vehicle in accord with VicRoads requirements.

On receipt of signed paperwork from permit scheme officer members must submit same to VicRoads (including RWC for New Members).

Advise JCCA and VicRoads of any change of contact details.

New CPS Application Requirements

Applicants must allow sufficient time for application forms to be processed by an authorised JCCA officer and include the following:

Current Roadworthy Certificate Recent High Quality images of the car (VicRoads requirement) emailed to cpsvic@jcca.org.au

.        Front and rear of car Drivers Side of car.
.        Driving Position with Drivers door agar.
.        Engine Number.
.        Chassis Number

A completed VicRoads vehicle eligibility and standards declaration for club permits form.

Any additional documentation mandated by VicRoads Vehicle Assessment Signatory Scheme (VASS) for modified cars

Stamped self addressed envelope.
